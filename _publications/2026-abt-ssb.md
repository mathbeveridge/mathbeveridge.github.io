We consider a family of binary triangular arrays, called approval ballot triangles (ABTs), that are in bijection with 
totally symmetric self-complementary plane partitions (TSSCPPs). These triangles correspond to a  ballot process in which 
voters select their collection of approved candidates rather than voting for a single person.  We situate ABTs within the 
ballot problem literature and then show that a strict-sense ballot can be decomposed into a list of sequentially compatible ABTs.
